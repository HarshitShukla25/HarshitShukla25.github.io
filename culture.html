Culture

Get to know the E-Cell and the culture around here

Entrepreneurship Culture at IIT BHU is vibrant and it is very old. Many of our alumni are successful entrepreneurs. Also, E-Cell is there in IIT BHU to promote startup culture with the motto “Help will always be given to those who seek it”. Throughout the year, E-Cell, IIT BHU organizes startup events and at the same provides a platform for the budding startups to launch their product into the market.

NCL (Indian Institute of Technology (Banaras Hindu University) Varanasi)
Like everyone needs a home to reside in, a startup requires space to grow and nurture itself. Hence, E Cell It works in collaboration with NCL (Northern Coalfields Limited) IIT BHU chapter .


MCIIE

Malaviya Centre for Innovation Incubation and Entrepreneurship(MCIIE) is the Purvanchal’s only Business incubation center and top 19th in the country. This center was established in 2008 as a private non-profit society. Its purpose is to stimulate the growth of technology-based incubating companies to get a properly competitive market and multidisciplinary exposure in regards to the existing market and fulfillment of this mission.

R-ABI

The Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojna(RKVY) is an important scheme of the Government Of India, Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare, and MoA&FW sanctioned to establish an RKVY-RAFTAAR Agri-Business Incubator(R-ABI) at IIT BHU. It aims at strengthening infrastructure in agriculture and allied areas to promote agro-preneurship and agribusiness by financial support. Under this scheme, new agro-based business incubators will be established as well as existing agribusiness incubators strengthened as R-ABI with need-based infrastructure, equipment, and manpower.

Cisco thingQbator

"Cisco thingQbator" is a network of maker-spaces in partner Universities where students can learn about digital technologies in a hands-on environment, turn their ideas into working prototypes, and in the process come up with local solutions to local problems.

Business Club

The Business Club is a de-facto place where the confluence of Economics, Consulting, Data Analytics, and Finance transpires. The Club promotes the learning and understanding of the same via group discussions, brainstorming sessions, case studies, debates, and stock simulations. Our vision is to collaborate with the consulting clubs of top B-schools, make premium knowledge platforms accessible, enrich skills through lectures from various speakers, and projects to hone the skills by testing them on real-life problems.

● "Bizstorm", a weekly session conducted by the Club, discusses the advancements on the business frontier.

● “Mavericks”, an initiative, wherein eminent pundits of business share the insights they have developed in their course of finding their passion. People like Rohan Jain, a senior associate at BCG, Hitanshu Gandhi, VP Business, Manufacturing Brands at Magicpin, Aviral Bhatnagar, Junior VC at Guild Capital among others.

● “The Case Book”, the signature project of the Club, contains all the case studies that were conducted in a year. With The Consulting Crew, a faction of students interested in managerial and consulting jobs, the Club aims at better preparation of students for placements in the same domain.

Club has been publishing content through its Facebook and Linkedin page regularly. Some of the achievements of the Club and its members include two gold and one silver in Inter IIT'19. Our team was among the top six Regional Finalists in Dubai in Hult Prize'17 and club members were the campus winners of Hult Prize'19 and proceeded to the Regionals. The Club had a clean sweep at all the business events at IIT Guwahati's Techniche’18, claiming a podium finish in all the events. The Club successfully organized two editions of BASH, its flagship event. The second edition though conducted through online modes saw an overwhelming participation of 150+ first yearites. The club successfully laid the foundation of ShARE IIT(BHU) Chapter in May 2019. The organization aims at directing students towards consulting. In January 2019, Satya Nadella, the CEO of Microsoft, appreciated the Club's work by saying "you are doing important work to shape what comes next for all of us. Good luck and thank you so much".
